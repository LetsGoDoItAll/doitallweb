The Science Behind Bravery

Bravery is not the absence of fear but a deliberate choice to act despite it. Neuroscience reveals that courage is deeply connected to how our brains regulate fear and process decisions. The amygdala, often associated with the "fight or flight" response, plays a key role in detecting threats, while the prefrontal cortex helps us override impulsive reactions and make thoughtful, courageous choices.

Actionable Steps to Cultivate Dedication

  1. Pause and Reflect: Ask yourself what your heart truly desires and write it down.
